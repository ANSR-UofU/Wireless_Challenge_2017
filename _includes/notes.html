Notes from Conversations

Amateur Radio

The Amateur Radio Emergency Service (ARES) is in charge of providing communication support for government and non-governmental disaster response organizations during times of disaster using the Amateur Radio bands.

CERT

Response Teams use cell phones (if available) and ARES members to relay information when responding. When they respond, they provide light search and rescue and medical aid.

American Red Cross

People arriving at shelters complete registration papers which includes information for the Safe and Well program administered by the Red Cross. ARES members are used to provide communications when other infrastructure is compromised.

S.A.F.E. Neighborhoods

S.A.F.E. Neighborhoods is a program that started in Salt Lake County but is being replicated by other counties in Utah. As part of this program, elementary schools are used as hubs during disasters. Response kits are prepositioned at the elementary schools. After a disaster happens, the expectation is people will go to their nearest elementary school. Once there, people will complete Safe and Well information and try to self-organize. The plan is to also use the elementary schools as communication and transportation hubs.
